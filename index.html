Biography

Emilio Mwai Kibaki was a Kenyan politician who served as the third President of Kenya from 2002 to 2013. He was born in Gatuyaini village, Othaya, Nyeri County, in the central highlands of Kenya. Kibaki was an economics and history student at Makerere University in Uganda and later attended the London School of Economics.

Before becoming president, Kibaki served as Member of Parliament for Donholm (now Makadara) constituency, Minister of Finance, and Vice President under Daniel arap Moi. He was instrumental in establishing the Kenya School of Monetary Studies and the Kenya Revenue Authority.

"The resources of our country should be utilized to support the development projects that will benefit the people and not to enrich a few individuals."

Legacy

Mwai Kibaki is remembered as a transformative leader who presided over significant economic growth and development in Kenya. His introduction of free primary education allowed millions of Kenyan children to access schooling. The new constitution promulgated during his presidency established a more democratic framework for governance.

Despite challenges, including the post-election violence of 2007-2008, Kibaki's presidency is largely viewed as a period of progress and modernization for Kenya. His economic policies stimulated growth and attracted foreign investment, while his calm and measured leadership style earned him respect across political divides.
